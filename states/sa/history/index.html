South Australia history

South Australia is the franchise pioneer: planned colony, early responsible government, landmark women's suffrage and a contemporary state-level First Nations Voice.

Last research run2026-05-08Australia/Brisbane

Basic path

What most people need first.

The basic South Australian story is a planned colony that built representative government and then led the world on women voting and standing for Parliament.

Advanced layer

The machinery underneath.

The advanced story is how franchise design, social reform and newer Voice architecture make SA a useful state for testing democratic inclusion.

State difference: SA is where democratic inclusion often appears early in Australian institutional history.

SA Voting rights

1894 to 1896

Women win the right to vote and stand

South Australian women gained the right to vote in 1894 and first voted in the 1896 election.

Voting rights Reform
Advanced layer

Advanced: The legislation also allowed women to stand for Parliament, making SA one of the most important global franchise milestones.

State difference: SA is the benchmark state for modelling franchise expansion as democratic infrastructure.
