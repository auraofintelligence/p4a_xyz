A commuter-level clip about toll roads that never seem to untoll, then a hard turn into public assets, long contracts and fair-term infrastructure deals.

Clip brief

The everyday gripe

Someone speaks straight to camera while having a piss: why do Australians keep paying for infrastructure that was meant to become easier, cheaper or public again? The joke is ordinary. The trapdoor is a public register of deals that should not be able to hide in legal fog.

Description

BaitTolls are one of the few policy topics people can feel in their bank account every week.
SwitchThe clip opens into long contracts, public-private leases and renegotiation windows.
SystemPublic assets need public memory: who controls them, who profits, when they expire, and what options exist.
